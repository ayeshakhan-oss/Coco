## Who Is Taleemabad?

Taleemabad is a Pakistani EdTech company building AI-powered, curriculum-aligned digital
learning for government and low-fee private schools. Their mission is to make quality
education accessible at scale — targeting the 23 million out-of-school children in Pakistan.

**Founded:** ~2019–2020 (Islamabad, Pakistan)
**CEO / Founder:** Haroon Yasin (Georgetown University Qatar alumnus)
**Chief Program Officer:** Sabeena Abbasi
**Headquarters:** Islamabad, Pakistan

---

## What They Build

### Core Product — Digital Curriculum
- K–5 (Grades 1–5) digital curriculum aligned to Pakistan's **Single National Curriculum (SNC)**
- Structured lessons in Urdu, English, Maths, General Knowledge, Islamiat
- Delivered via: Learning Management System (LMS) app, TV broadcast, radio broadcast
- Includes **teacher training** so educators can deliver lessons effectively
- **Rumi** — an AI teaching assistant embedded in the platform

### Distribution Channels
| Channel | Reach |
|---|---|
| Mobile app (LMS) | 1.5M users |
| TV broadcast | ~8.5M students/week (partnered with PTV and educational channels) |
| Radio broadcast | Part of the 8.5M weekly figure above |
| Partner schools | 170 schools (private + government) |

This multi-channel approach is intentional — most families in underserved areas have TVs
or radios but NOT smartphones or reliable internet.

## Government Partnerships

Taleemabad has meaningful government relationships — rare for Pakistani EdTech startups:

- **Ministry of Education (MoE):** Formal partnership. SNC endorsement. Planned expansion
  to 310 public schools across ICT (Islamabad Capital Territory).
- **Pilot:** Started with 6 ICT government schools. Expansion to 310 is underway.
- **SNC Alignment:** Taleemabad curriculum is officially aligned to the Single National
  Curriculum — required for any school using government-recognised content.
- **Provincial engagement:** Active in Balochistan and potentially other provinces.

This means Fundraising / Partnerships candidates MUST understand government navigation —
how to work with MoE officials, SEDs (School Education Departments), district education
officers. This is NOT the same as corporate B2B sales.

## Strategic Context for Screening

### Why This Matters for Fundraising / Partnerships Roles

1. **Donor landscape is EdTech + development, not WASH or humanitarian.**
   Relevant donors: Malala Fund, Sorenson, impact VCs, bilateral donors who fund education
   (FCDO, USAID, GPE, UNICEF), and Pakistani philanthropists. A WASH-sector fundraiser
   (even if exceptional) lacks the donor relationship capital Taleemabad needs.

2. **Education system literacy is non-negotiable.**
   Taleemabad interacts with MoE, Provincial Education Departments, SNC committees, and
   school principals. Candidates who have never navigated these relationships will have
   a steep learning curve. TCF, READ Foundation, Teach For Pakistan alumni have this context.

3. **Government navigation nuance matters.**
   Their biggest growth lever (310 schools) runs THROUGH the government. Anyone in
   partnerships must understand how government procurement, MoU signing, and school-level
   buy-in works in Pakistani public sector. This is different from donor relations.

4. **Budget reality: lean organisation.**
   $2.3M seed round for a company of Taleemabad's reach means salaries are likely
   below commercial market. Strong candidates from large multilaterals or banks may
   have unrealistic salary expectations. Flag this proactively.

5. **Mission alignment is a real filter.**
   Candidates from purely commercial sectors (FMCG, banking, telecom) rarely succeed
   in mission-driven organisations unless they have a demonstrated track record of
   pivoting. Look for explicit evidence of values alignment — not just "I care about
   education" statements, but actual time volunteered, education sector roles, or
   academic work in the space.

## Implications for Future CV Screening

### Must-Check Lenses (beyond standard JD match)

When screening ANY Taleemabad role, always evaluate:

1. **Education System Literacy** — Has the candidate worked in or with schools, MEDs,
   SEDs, or education-focused organisations? Not just adjacent public sector.

2. **Government Navigation** — Can they handle MoU negotiations, government procurement,
   or school-level relationship management? Not corporate sales / CSR.

3. **Mission Alignment Signal** — Is there evidence beyond slogans? Volunteer work,
   education publications, sector commitment.

4. **Donor Landscape Fit** — For fundraising roles: do they know impact investors,
   bilateral education donors, Pakistani philanthropy? Not WASH or humanitarian donors.

5. **Lean Org Mentality** — Taleemabad is ~50-person org, not a large UN agency.
   Candidates accustomed to large institutional bureaucracy may struggle. Look for
   "built from scratch," "wore many hats," "0→1" evidence.

6. **Pakistan-Specific Knowledge** — SNC, NEMIS, district education systems, province-
   specific education dynamics. International experience is a bonus, not a substitute.

## Project: Talent Acquisition Agent

### Purpose
Automates CV screening: ingest CVs → score vs JD + budget → ranked report → notify hiring manager.

### Pipeline
1. Read JD from `jobs.jd_text` (fallback: `jobs.description`)
2. Read CVs from `candidates.resume_data` (Base64 PDF → decode → parse → OCR if needed)
3. Score with 7-dimension framework (see skills/cv-screening.md)
4. Apply Taleemabad-specific lenses from this file
5. Generate 7-section HTML report (see skills/report-generation.md)
6. Email to hiring manager (see skills/email-notification.md)

### Data Sources (confirmed)
- CVs: Neon DB, `candidates.resume_data` (Base64-encoded PDF)
- JDs: Neon DB, `jobs.jd_text` / `jobs.description`
- Budget: Neon DB, `jobs.min_budget` / `jobs.max_budget` (mostly NULL — flag as "budget TBD")
- Hiring manager email: Neon DB, `users` table (via `jobs.hiring_manager` FK)

### Active Jobs Database
- Filter active roles: `WHERE job_status = 'Active'`
- Applications table: `ai_overall_score`, `ai_recommendation`, `ai_screened_at`, etc.
