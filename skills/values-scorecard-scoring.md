## Objective

Score candidates on Taleemabad's 6 core values using the interview transcript provided by Ayesha. Use a structured rating system (+/+/-/-) to produce a clear PASS/OUT verdict and GWC assessment. **Do NOT submit on Markaz automatically.** Always ask Ayesha for approval before submitting. Confirm candidate identity and position before submission. Provide interview process feedback to help Ayesha refine her interviewing technique.

**System:** People Analyzer (Markaz integration)

**Key Change (2026-04-10):** This SOP now includes 7 mandatory steps, including approval-before-submitting, candidate/position confirmation, fairness in transcript interpretation, and interview feedback.

## The 6 Values to Assess

1. **Don't Walk Away** — commitment to mission and communities; persistence through difficulty
2. **All for One** — team collaboration; lifting teammates; showing up for the collective
3. **Continuously Improve** — growth mindset; seeking feedback; helping team develop better ways of working
4. **Courageous Conversations** — ability to have hard conversations; honesty; pushing back on group dynamics
5. **Don't Hold On Too Tight** — flexibility; adaptability; letting go of being right; learning from mistakes
6. **Practice Joy** — bringing energy and warmth; finding lightness in hard work; sustaining teams emotionally

---

## The Rating System (3 Options Per Value)

For each value, assign ONE of these ratings:

- **+** (Plus) = Candidate exhibits this value clearly. Evidence is specific, compelling, and directly observed in the interview.

- **+/-** (Plus-Minus) = Candidate is inconsistent on this value. Some moments show it, some moments don't. Or the evidence is present but weak/unclear.

- **-** (Minus) = Candidate does NOT exhibit this value. Evidence is absent or contradicts the value. Or candidate actively demonstrated the opposite.

---

## The Pass/Out Logic (NON-NEGOTIABLE)

**PASS:** Zero minuses AND ≤2 plus-minuses

Acceptable combinations:
- 6 pluses = automatic PASS
- 5 pluses + 1 plus-minus = PASS
- 4 pluses + 2 plus-minuses = PASS
- Any configuration with zero minuses and ≤2 plus-minuses = PASS

**OUT:** Any minus OR ≥3 plus-minuses

Automatic OUT conditions:
- Even one minus = OUT (regardless of other ratings)
- Three or more plus-minuses = OUT (even with no minuses)

## GWC Assessment (Gets it / Wants it / Capacity)

**When:** Only evaluated for candidates who PASS the values round.

**Three questions to answer (all must be YES):**

1. **Gets it** — Does the candidate demonstrate understanding of Taleemabad's mission and values? Do they "get" what we're doing?

2. **Wants it** — Does the candidate genuinely want to work here? Is this a real fit, or are they just looking for a job?

3. **Capacity** — Does the candidate have the actual capacity (bandwidth, skill, maturity) to show up on these values day-to-day in the role?

**If all 3 = YES:** Candidate is READY for Right Seat interview.

**If any = NO:** Candidate is CONDITIONAL — flag for specific GWC probe or reassessment before Right Seat.

## The Scorecard Structure (4 Evidence Columns)

For each value, populate ALL FOUR columns:

1. **Deep-Dive Evidence** — a substantial moment from the interview where the value was tested or demonstrated. Usually from a structured question. Cite specific quote or behavior.

2. **Curve-Ball Evidence** — a moment where the interviewer asked an unexpected or challenging question. How did the candidate respond? What did this reveal about their value alignment?

3. **Micro-Case Evidence** — a small moment, gesture, or comment that revealed the value. Can be a brief exchange, not necessarily a long story.

4. **Rating** — assign +, +/-, or - based on the weight of all three evidence types.

**Key rule:** Never leave evidence blank. Every value must have observations in all three columns, even if one column has less substantial evidence. If not observed, state "Not directly evident in interview."

## Step-by-Step Scoring Process (7 Critical Steps)

### Step 1: Read the Transcript Fully

**Ayesha usually provides the interview transcript.** Read it completely before scoring. Don't skim or jump to conclusions. Understand the full context, flow, and nuance of the conversation.

---

### Step 2: Score Based on Transcript Evidence

Existing scoring methodology is largely correct. Use the 4-evidence-column system (Deep-Dive, Curve-Ball, Micro-Case, Rating) to assess value alignment.

**Key:** Every observation must be tied to actual quotes or paraphrases from the transcript, not assumptions.

---

### Step 3: Ask Before Submitting on Markaz

**Do NOT submit automatically.** After preparing the scorecard (all 6 values scored, PASS/OUT logic verified, GWC assessed if applicable), explicitly ask Ayesha:

> "Should I go and submit this on Markaz or not?"

Wait for approval before submitting. This prevents accidental submissions and catches errors before data is locked in.

---

### Step 4: Ensure Correct Candidate and Correct Position

**A recent mistake was submitting under the wrong candidate and wrong position.** This is now mandatory:

1. **Confirm candidate identity** — verify the name, email, and application ID match the interview transcript
2. **Confirm position** — verify the role/position the candidate applied for
3. **Fill the scorecard only against the correct person and role** — use Markaz filtering to ensure you're entering data for the right candidate + role combination
4. **Before submitting:** Double-check candidate name and position one final time

---

### Step 5: Personal Examples Are Acceptable

Candidates may give examples from personal life, not only professional life. **These are acceptable if they genuinely fit the value being assessed.**

Example: A candidate discussing how they resolved a conflict in a family context can demonstrate "Courageous Conversations" if the example is genuine and the reasoning is sound.

**Rule:** Evaluate the example on its merit (does it show the value?), not on the context (work vs. personal). Personal examples are valid evidence.

---

### Step 6: Be Somewhat More Lenient When Needed

While reading transcripts, **consider whether any confusion or issue may have come from the interviewer's phrasing.** Be context-aware and fair in your assessment.

**This means:**
- If a candidate seemed confused by a question, don't penalize the value rating — look at what they actually demonstrated
- If the interviewer rephrased a question mid-way, use the clearest version to assess the response
- If timing or flow seemed off, give the candidate the benefit of the doubt
- Rate on what the candidate showed, not on interview logistics

**Example:** If Ayesha asked a complex multi-part question and the candidate answered only one part, that's an interviewer phrasing issue, not evidence the candidate lacks the value. Rate based on the response to the clearer interpretation.

---

### Step 7: Give Ayesha Interview Feedback Too

**Coco should also highlight:**

- Where Ayesha could phrase questions better (e.g., "This question was ambiguous; consider rephrasing as...")
- Where a response from the interviewer may have been harsh or mistaken (e.g., "Your follow-up comment seemed to put the candidate on the defensive")
- Where the interview process itself could improve (e.g., "The candidate seemed uncomfortable with the pacing; consider adding a breath moment")

**This feedback should be explicit and constructive.** Provide it in a separate section after the scorecard:

```
## Interview Process Feedback for Ayesha

1. **Question Clarity:** [What could be improved]
2. **Interviewer Tone/Approach:** [What went well or could be gentler]
3. **Process Improvement:** [Suggestions for flow, timing, environment]
```

This helps Ayesha refine her interview technique over time and ensures fairness to future candidates.

## Common Mistakes

1. **Submitting without asking Ayesha first** — "I'll just go ahead and submit." Wrong. Always ask: "Should I go and submit this on Markaz or not?" Wait for approval. (NEW — learned 2026-04-10)

2. **Submitting under wrong candidate or wrong position** — Recent mistake: submitted under wrong candidate name or wrong job ID. Always confirm both before submitting. (NEW — learned 2026-04-10)

3. **Rating too high on incomplete evidence** — "I didn't see this value tested, but I assume they have it." You can only rate + based on observed evidence from transcript.

4. **Confusing + with +/-** — a candidate shows a value once but inconsistently. That's +/-, not +. Reserve + for consistent, clear evidence.

5. **Letting one strong value override a weak one** — "They're amazing at Continuously Improve, so I'll give them a + on Courageous Conversations too." Each value stands alone. Rate each independently.

6. **Ignoring a minus with "but they passed other values"** — One minus = OUT. Period. This is non-negotiable.

7. **Penalizing candidates for interviewer phrasing issues** — If Ayesha's question was confusing and the candidate misunderstood, that's not a value gap. Rate based on what they actually demonstrated. (NEW — be more lenient, 2026-04-10)

8. **Rejecting personal examples** — "This is from their personal life, not work." Personal examples are valid if they genuinely fit the value. Don't dismiss them. (NEW — 2026-04-10)

9. **Assessing GWC before finalizing the PASS/OUT verdict** — finalize ratings first, then check logic. Only assess GWC if PASS is confirmed.

10. **Leaving GWC blank** — if PASS, you must assess all 3 questions. Blank GWC = incomplete scorecard.

11. **Rating based on CV background, not interview evidence** — "They have 10 years of experience, so +" is wrong. Rate based on what you observed in the interview, not the resume.

12. **Plus-minus inflation** — +/- should be ~20–30% of your ratings. If you're rating 4+ plus-minuses per candidate, your threshold is too low.

13. **Evidence that's too vague** — "They showed good teamwork" is not evidence. "When asked about a time they supported a teammate, they described absorbing a colleague's work during a crisis" is evidence.

14. **Not providing interview feedback to Ayesha** — Scorecard is submitted but no feedback on interview quality. Always flag: question phrasing issues, harsh interviewer moments, process improvements. (NEW — required, 2026-04-10)

15. **Submitting before double-checking the pass/out logic** — before hitting submit, verify: count minuses (should be 0) + count plus-minuses (should be ≤2) = PASS. If any minus or ≥3 plus-minuses = OUT. Don't submit without this verification.
