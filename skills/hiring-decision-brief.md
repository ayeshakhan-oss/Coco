## Objective

Create a highly detailed hiring brief for leadership and hiring managers, combining complete pipeline progress (candidate counts at each stage) and detailed candidate recommendations based on resume, values, and case study evaluations.

**Audience:** Hiring managers + Leadership team

**Quality expectation:** Extremely detailed, complete, with nothing important missing.

**Email subject line:** `Hiring for [Position Name] – Decision Brief`

---

## Data Sources (Non-Negotiable: Check All Three)

This skill requires Coco to review three separate sources to ensure accuracy and completeness:

1. **Markaz** — candidate status, shortlist, application pipeline, case study submissions
2. **Ayesha's Gmail** — interview invites, case study debrief emails, communication history
3. **Ayesha's Calendar** — booked interview slots, confirmed attendees, scheduling status

**Rule:** Never rely on one source alone. Cross-check all three to get the complete picture.

---

## 10-Step SOP (Data Collection & Assembly)

### Step 1: Count Shortlisted Candidates

**Action:** Check Markaz and identify how many candidates were shortlisted for this position.

**What to look for:**
- Applications with status = "shortlisted" or equivalent
- Total count of candidates advanced from CV screening

**Document:** "X candidates shortlisted from CV screening"

---

### Step 2: Count Values Interview Invites Sent

**Action:** Check Ayesha's Gmail for emails with subject lines containing:
- "Invitation for Values Interview"
- "Zero In Call"
- Similar phrasing indicating values interview invite

**What to count:** Number of unique invites sent (not replies or reminders).

**Document:** "Y invitations sent for values interviews"

---

### Step 3: Count Calendar Bookings

**Action:** Check Ayesha's Calendar for bookings labeled or titled:
- "Zero In Call"
- "Values Interview"
- Similar calendar event titles

**What to identify:** Who actually booked a values interview slot (vs. who was just invited).

**Document:** "Z candidates booked values interview slots"

---

### Step 4: Count Completed Values Calls

**Action:** Review calendar for past values call bookings (check date vs. today).

**What to count:** How many values interviews have already taken place (completed, not pending).

**Document:** "A values calls completed"

---

### Step 5: Count Values Pass/Fail Outcomes

**Action:** Check Markaz for values scorecard submissions and verdicts.

**What to report:**
- How many candidates CLEARED values (PASS verdict)
- How many did NOT clear values (OUT verdict)

**Document:**
- "B candidates cleared values interview"
- "C candidates did not clear values interview"

---

### Step 6: Count Case Studies Sent

**Action:** Check both Markaz (case study submissions status) and Gmail (case study send emails).

**What to count:** How many candidates were sent case study assignments (only those who passed values).

**Document:** "D case studies sent to candidates"

---

### Step 7: Count Case Study Debrief Invites

**Action:** Check Gmail for case study debrief invitation emails.

**What to look for:**
- Emails inviting candidates to discuss/debrief their case study
- Subject lines like "Case Study Debrief", "Let's Discuss Your Submission", etc.

**Document:** "E case study debrief invites sent"

---

### Step 8: Count Case Study Debrief Bookings

**Action:** Check both Gmail (responses confirming attendance) and Ayesha's Calendar (booked debrief slots).

**What to identify:**
- Who booked debrief slots (confirmed attendance)
- Who was invited but did NOT book (pending or declined)

**Document:**
- "F debriefs booked/confirmed"
- "G debriefs pending (invited but not yet booked)"

---

### Step 9: Create Header Summary with Pipeline Boxes

**Header Format:** Dark navy background (#1a2a3a), white text, title: "Hiring for [Position Name] – Decision Brief"

**Stat Boxes (10 colored boxes, horizontal layout):**

1. **Shortlisted** (red) — X candidates
2. **Invited to Values** (blue) — Y invitations sent
3. **Booked Values** (orange) — Z candidates with calendar bookings
4. **Completed Values** (green) — A calls completed
5. **Cleared Values** (teal) — B candidates PASS
6. **Failed Values** (pink) — C candidates OUT
7. **Case Studies Sent** (purple) — D assignments
8. **Debrief Invites** (yellow) — E invites sent
9. **Debriefs Booked** (gray) — F confirmed / G pending
10. **Decision Status** (navy) — e.g., "Panel decision pending" or "Offer ready"

**Non-negotiable:** All 10 boxes must be present. Numbers must be accurate and cross-checked across sources.

---

### Step 10: Provide Top Candidate Recommendations

**Action:** Based on all three components (resume, values scorecard, case study evaluation), identify and describe top candidates.

**Structure per candidate:**

1. **Name (hyperlinked to Google Drive CV)**
2. **Current Role & Company**
3. **Resume Strengths** — 2–3 bullet points tied to JD requirements
4. **Values Assessment** — PASS/OUT verdict with GWC status
5. **Case Study Performance** — Tier and score (e.g., "Tier 1, 87%")
6. **Overall Recommendation** — Framed as a recommendation, NOT an absolute "hire this person"
   - Example: "Strong candidate for Right Seat conversation. [Specific reason tied to all three components]"
   - NOT: "Hire this person immediately."

**Important:** Frame recommendations as professional suggestions, not directives. Hiring manager and leadership make final decisions.

## Content Structure (4-Part Email)

### Part A: Header & Pipeline Stat Boxes
- Navy header with title
- 10 colored stat boxes showing pipeline counts at each stage
- Clear visual summary of where candidates are in the process

### Part B: Pipeline Summary (Grouped by Status)

Organize all candidates by their current status:

1. **Values PASS – Debrief Pending** — list with debrief date or "Awaiting confirmation"
2. **Values PASS – Debrief Completed** — list with debrief outcome/notes
3. **Values OUT** — list with brief reason (value gap, GWC misalignment, etc.)
4. **Case Study In (Not Yet Evaluated)** — list if applicable
5. **Case Study Pending (Not Yet Submitted)** — candidates who are overdue
6. **Did Not Advance** — candidates screened out at CV stage

### Part C: Top Candidate Recommendations

**Leading Candidates Section:**
- Name (hyperlinked)
- Current role
- Resume strengths
- Values verdict + GWC
- Case study performance
- Recommendation (framed as suggestion, not directive)

### Part D: Next Steps & Timeline

- What decisions are pending (panel decision, offer approval, etc.)
- Who needs to be contacted next
- Timeline for final decisions
- Any blockers or items requiring leadership attention

## Non-Negotiable Rules

1. **Check all three sources** — Markaz + Gmail + Calendar. Never rely on one alone.

2. **Pipeline counts must be accurate** — Cross-check numbers across sources. If discrepancies, investigate before reporting.

3. **Every candidate name must be hyperlinked** — All sections (Pipeline Summary, Leading Candidates, etc.) must have clickable CV links to Google Drive.

4. **No assumptions on candidate status** — Only report what you can verify in Markaz, Gmail, or Calendar. If uncertain, flag to Ayesha.

5. **Recommendations are suggestions, not directives** — Use phrases: "Strong candidate for...", "Worth considering for...", "Recommended for next conversation". Never: "Hire this person" or "This is the right choice."

6. **Nothing important missing** — This is the comprehensive brief for leadership. Don't omit candidates, dates, or decisions. Be thorough.

7. **All stat boxes required** — 10 boxes showing pipeline flow. Missing any box = incomplete brief.

8. **Pipeline organization matters** — Group candidates by status clearly. Leadership needs to understand where each person is.

9. **Email format is inline HTML** — Use v8 design (blue headings, Georgia serif, justified text). No PDF attachment.

10. **Recipients:** TO = Hiring Manager, CC = hiring@taleemabad.com + ayesha.khan@taleemabad.com + any additional stakeholders user specifies.

11. **Always ask for approval first** — PILOT_MODE = True (send to Ayesha + Jawwad only). Get approval before live send.

## Common Mistakes

1. **Missing stat box** — only 9 of 10 boxes included. Brief feels incomplete to leadership.

2. **Numbers don't match across sources** — Markaz says 5 shortlisted, Gmail shows 4 invites sent. Didn't reconcile.

3. **Candidate names not hyperlinked** — Pipeline section lists names but no CV links. Leadership can't quickly review profiles.

4. **Recommendations sound like directives** — "Hire this person" or "This is the right choice" instead of "Strong candidate for Right Seat conversation."

5. **Missing candidates** — Some candidates from earlier stages not mentioned in Pipeline Summary. Leadership doesn't know what happened to them.

6. **Vague decision status** — "Awaiting decision" too soft. Should say: "Panel decision required by [date]" or "Offer approval pending CFO sign-off."

7. **No Next Steps section** — brief ends abruptly. Leadership doesn't know what happens now.

8. **PILOT_MODE = False immediately** — sent straight to hiring manager without Ayesha review.

9. **Calendar not checked** — only used Markaz. Missed candidates who booked but aren't yet reflected in DB.

10. **Recommendations based on one component only** — "Great resume, so hire them" ignores values/case study results.
